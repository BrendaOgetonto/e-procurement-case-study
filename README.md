# E-Procurement System — Product Case Study

**Company:** Kinetic Technology Ltd  
**Role:** Product Manager  
**Timeline:** 2024 – Present  
**Status:** Live in production

---

## Overview

A digital procurement platform that replaced a largely paper-based and email-driven procurement process. The system covers vendor onboarding, RFQ (Request for Quotation) management, purchase order processing, and approval workflows — bringing speed, transparency, and control to the full procurement cycle.

---

## The Problem

The client's procurement team was managing vendor relationships and purchasing through a combination of email, phone calls, and physical documents. This created delays at every stage of the process and made it difficult to maintain audit trails or enforce approval controls.

Key pain points:
- Vendor onboarding was slow — requiring back-and-forth document collection over email
- RFQs were sent manually and responses tracked in spreadsheets
- No centralised visibility into what had been ordered, approved, or delivered
- Approval chains were unclear — procurement decisions were sometimes made without proper sign-off
- Month-end reconciliation was time-consuming due to scattered records

## Discovery & Research

Procurement is a two-sided workflow — internal buyers and external vendors both needed to be considered. I ran separate discovery tracks for each.

**Internal (buyers & approvers):**
- Process walk-throughs with the procurement team
- Interviews with department heads who raised purchase requests
- Finance review to understand approval and reconciliation needs

**External (vendors):**
- Interviews with 6 existing vendors to understand their experience of the current process
- Identified onboarding friction as the single biggest vendor complaint

**Key insight:** Vendors were dropping off during onboarding not because the requirements were too strict, but because the process was opaque — they didn't know what was needed or where they stood. A self-service onboarding portal with clear status visibility was the most impactful first feature we could build.

## Solution & Key Features

| Feature | Problem it solved |
|---|---|
| Vendor self-service onboarding portal | Vendors submit documents online; status visible at all times |
| Digital RFQ management | RFQs sent, tracked, and compared in one place |
| Automated approval workflows | Purchase requests routed to the right approvers automatically |
| Purchase order management | POs generated, sent, and tracked digitally |
| Spend visibility dashboard | Real-time view of committed and actual spend |
| Audit trail | Every action logged — full compliance and accountability |
| Vendor performance tracking | Delivery times, quality scores, and response rates tracked over time |

---

## Prioritisation Approach

The procurement team had a long list of requirements. I ran a **value vs. risk prioritisation workshop** with stakeholders to distinguish what was genuinely blocking them today from what was "nice to have."

This surfaced a clear Phase 1 focus: fix the vendor onboarding bottleneck and digitise the RFQ process. Everything else — performance tracking, advanced analytics — moved to Phase 2.

**Release phasing:**
- **Phase 1:** Vendor onboarding portal, RFQ management, basic approval workflows
- **Phase 2:** Spend dashboard, vendor performance module, ERP integration

## What I Learned

**Designing for two user types requires two discovery tracks.** Early on, I almost designed the onboarding portal entirely from the internal team's perspective. Bringing vendors into the discovery process revealed a completely different set of friction points — and the solution that emerged was better for both sides.

**Workflow transparency is a feature.** For both vendors and internal approvers, the most-requested thing wasn't a new capability — it was just knowing *where things stood*. Status visibility, email notifications, and a clear dashboard reduced follow-up chasing significantly and built trust in the system faster than any other feature.
